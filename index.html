Defending Free Speech in Texas

The Texas Right to Boycott Network is an alliance of over a dozen state and local civil liberties, human rights, faith-based and community groups. The Network is dedicated to preserving the right to boycott and divest from countries and corporations involved in human rights violations and abuses. Boycotting and divesting are of free speech afforded the highest level of constitutional protection by the United States Supreme Court.

 Reflecting a disturbing nationwide trend, Texas legislatures are attacking the right to boycott by attempting to pass legislation that would suppress boycott, divestment and sanctions (BDS) campaigns for Palestinian rights. In particular, lawmakers have submitted three anti-boycott bills to the 2017 legislative session. These bills include Senate Bills 134 and 29 and House Bill 89. If passed, the legislation would prohibit the state from making contracts with businesses that boycott Israel.

 BDS is a grassroots, human rights movement modeled on the international boycott of apartheid South Africa. It asks the international community to financially, academically and culturally divest from Israel until Israel ends its Occupation of Arab lands, recognizes the right of Arab-Palestinian citizens of Israel to full equality, and respects, protects and promotes the rights of Palestinian refugees to return to their homes as stipulated by the United Nations. It also asks the international community to divest from companies, Israeli or otherwise, that profit from the Occupation.   

Whatever your views on Israel, Palestine, or BDS, anti-boycott measures should be of concern, because they threaten our right to take collective action against injustice.
